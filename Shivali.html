Shivali Sood

Running for SSMU Representative

Hi everyone! My name is Shivali Sood and I am more than excited to announce that I am running for one of the three positions of SUS Representative to the SSMU!

I am currently a first year U1 student in Microbiology and Immunology. I have lived in Montreal my whole life and am aware that it has its many flaws, with many of them due to lack of communication (orange cone dilemma, right?). That is why I believe that communication is key, and if elected, I will make sure the students under the Science Undergraduate Society (SUS) are well heard, whether it be concerning raising awareness for mental health or finding a common area for science students to study and/or mingle. My work and volunteer experience (e.g. Let’s Talk Science, SSMU, Hema-Quebec) have allowed me to develop skills that I believe as essential in taking on this position, such as active listening, note-taking and fast-learning. I feel very ready to take it on.

Personal Goals

This position is an excellent opportunity for me to be AWARE of everything that happens behind the scenes and to engage in fixing any problems that may arise. I really want to improve in my abilities to communicate ideas across platforms and between councils, but also in my capacity to look beyond the tip of my nose, into large-scale events that I was unaware of previously. If elected, I will make the most of this occasion to push my limits and give the most to my position as your representative.

Long Platform

My role as SSMU rep would be to make sure all opinions from science students are heard, without any bias. Impartiality and honesty are the foundations of how I plan on performing this role. I understand that part of my tasks would be to vote in motions that concern all the students under the SUS, whose best interests I seek, regardless of personal opinions. I intend on using all platforms available to me (e.g. social media, anonymous Google Forms, office hours, listservs, etc.) to learn about the concerns that science students may have and to be completely transparent with regards to any decisions that have been made. Relaying accurate information across both councils would be a priority. I plan on using my role to follow-up on the executives from both the SUS and SSMU to ensure that accountability, transparency and fair communication are present throughout the year. I know that many students are unaware of the motions around campus, and I plan on using a polling software to present the motions and understand student’s opinions concerning them (done before class in large science classrooms).

All-in-all, voting for me is voting for you. For your voice, your opinion and your ideas as a science undergraduate at McGill to be rightfully expressed. Thank you very much for reading, and don’t forget to vote!

Please feel free to contact me on Facebook or by mail should you have any questions or comments.
